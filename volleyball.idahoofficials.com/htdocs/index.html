Requirements

Varsity Volleyball Requirements

  • IDHSAA registration, including cardiac & concussion courses
  • 3rd District Officials Association registration
  • Oregon registration
  • State Rules Clinic
  • >=80% on NFHS exam
  • Recommendation of the Director and Commissioner

Varsity Post Season Ballot Eligibility

  • Varsity Official in good standing
  • Ten Hours of Training
    • 2 hours - Online Courses (cardiac, concussion, NFHS part II test)
    • 1 hour - State Rules meeting
    • 2-4 hours - Practical training (approved summer tournaments listed below, jamborees)
    • 3-5 hours - Association meetings
  • Officiate 10 varsity matches (tournaments count as 2 matches only)
  • Two association meetings

Varsity State Tournament Eligibility

  • Varsity Post Season Ballot eligible
  • Re-qualifying clinic (every three years)
  • 3 years varsity experience

Registration

2020 Registration Instructions


If you did not work High School Volleyball in the Third District in the 2018, contact Katie Homer at homerkatie@yahoo.com to secure access into Arbiter Sports, which is our scheduling software program. Katie is the Third District Volleyball Commissioner and she schedules all the matches. You must contact her in order to be on the schedule.


This is a reminder that we are independent contractors, not employees.

Step 1
- Register with the Third District Officials Association

Log into Arbiter to begin the registration process.

Select the “Registration” tab. Select the “2019 Volleyball” box. Next click on “Register”.

Read through the opening document. the fee will be $45.00. First year officials, new to District Three will initially be charged the full $45.00 fee and the Association will refund $30.00 at the conclusion of the first season of their first year of NFHS officiating.

Follow the steps in order and make sure you answer all the questions.

You must provide an appropriate, professional looking photo on Arbiter.

A background check will be completed on all applicants. Your consent is required in order for this to be completed.

Submit your payment information. Payment can be made via debit/credit card (Visa, Master Card) or by electronic check.


Step 2
- To register with the Idaho High School Activities Association online, go to their website,  www.idhsaa.org.

Click on Officials

On the dropdown menu, click Online Registration and follow the instructions.

Your e-mail address is mandatory for online registration. Your official ID number is not needed.

*Anyone failing to report they are a registered sex offender or have been convicted of a felony shall result in a suspension for a minimum of one (1) year.

We are located in Activity District III.

Registration Dates: August 2020


Step 3
– Register with Oregon School Activities Association (OSAA) at  osaa.arbitersports.com.

Follow these instructions carefully!!!

Click on "Register Now".

This will take you to the OSAA Registration Portal. Click on the Idaho State Seal for Idaho Officials.

At the 2019-2020 Idaho/District 3 Officials Registration page, select the “2019-2020 Volleyball Registration”.

The next page Welcomes you to their site and will walk you through the entire process, including payment of fees which is $25.50. Our Local Association is also referred to as 2019-2020 Malheur for Oregon purposes.


Step 4
– Complete the St. Luke’s Concussion Course and the NFHS Sudden Cardiac Arrest Course.

St. Luke's Concussion Course

NFHS Sudden Cardiac Arrest Course

If you took these certifications last school year Kim Allen will have a record and Reed Clayton will have access to the Arbiter site to be able to view the completed registrations and certifications.


Step 5
– Register with RefPay (Only for new officials) at  refpay.com. You will need to complete this step, in order to be paid, as most schools use this as their only form of payment.


Final Steps and Reminders!

Prior to being scheduled any games, each official must complete all registration requirements, pay all fees, and complete both required courses.

Each official is also required to attend a State Rules Clinic in order to certify to work High School volleyball.
